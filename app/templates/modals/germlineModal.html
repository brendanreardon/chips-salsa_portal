Optional Field: Germline Variants

If providing germline variants for upload, they should follow the guidelines set by the NCI's Mutation Annotation Format (MAF). We recommend HaplotypeCaller and Oncotator to generate this file. The Molecular Oncology Almanac specifically requires the following columns:

Hugo_Symbol, NCBI_Build, Chromosome, Start_position, End_position, Reference_Allele, Tumor_Seq_Allele1, Tumor_Seq_Allele2, Variant_Classification, Protein_Change, Tumor_Sample_Barcode, Matched_Norm_Sample_Barcode, Annotation_Transcript,
t_ref_count, t_alt_count

Note: Germline MAFs tend to be on the order of hundreds of megabytes in size, whereas most somatic MAFs are kilobytes. Your upload time will thus be a bit longer if providing a germline file. You can reduce upload time by shortening your input file to just the required columns.
